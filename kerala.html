Festivals in Kerala

Onam

Onam is Kerala's vibrant harvest festival celebrated with grandeur to honor the mythical King Mahabali, whose reign is remembered as a golden era of prosperity and equality. Rooted in Hindu mythology, it marks the annual visit of Mahabali from the underworld, symbolizing the values of unity and gratitude. Observed in the Malayalam month of Chingam (August-September), Onam is celebrated with colorful floral carpets (pookkalams), the grand Onam Sadya feast, traditional boat races, and cultural performances like Kathakali and Pulikali. Vishu

Vishu is a traditional festival celebrated in Kerala and parts of Karnataka, marking the Malayalam New Year in April. It symbolizes prosperity and is centered around the Vishukkani, an auspicious arrangement of items like rice, fruits, coins, and a mirror, viewed first thing in the morning. The day is filled with rituals, prayers, and family gatherings, and elders gift money (Vishukkaineetam) to younger members. Vishu signifies hope, renewal, and the start of a prosperous year.

Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram is one of the largest temple festivals in Kerala, held at the Vadakkunnathan Temple in Thrissur. The event is famous for its grand processions, elephant parades, and the traditional percussion performances known as Melam.

Theyyam

Theyyam is a vibrant ritualistic art form from Kerala, blending dance, music, and storytelling to honor deities and ancestors. Performed mainly in temples and sacred groves of North Kerala, it features elaborately dressed performers in striking makeup and headgear, embodying divine spirits. Rooted in folklore and tribal traditions, Theyyam reflects Kerala's cultural heritage and spiritual essence.
