Festivals in Tamil Nadu

Pongal

Pongal is the most significant harvest festival in Tamil Nadu, celebrated over four days in January. It includes Bhogi, Thai Pongal, Mattu Pongal, and Kaanum Pongal, where people worship the Sun God and thank nature for the harvest.

Tamil New Year

The Tamil New Year, or Puthandu, falls in mid-April and is marked by family gatherings, temple visits, and special feasts. It is a time for renewal and celebration in Tamil households, welcoming prosperity and happiness.

Thaipusam

Thaipusam is a religious festival dedicated to Lord Murugan, celebrated with processions and acts of devotion. Devotees often carry kavadis (decorated wooden structures) as a sign of penance and gratitude.

Karthigai Deepam

Karthigai Deepam, also known as the festival of lights, is celebrated by lighting lamps in homes and temples. The highlight is the lighting of the massive flame atop the Annamalai hill in Thiruvannamalai.
